CHILDREN RIGHTS AND MORAL DUTIES

all children rights are here
RIGHT TO EDUCATION:- The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, also known as the Right to Education (RTE) Act, was enacted in 2009. It guarantees free and compulsory education for children aged 6 to 14 years. Schools must meet specific infrastructure and teacher-student ratio requirements.

CHILD LABOUR:-
India has laws in place to prevent child style labor and protect the rights of working children. The Child Labor (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986, prohibits the employment of children below the age of 14 in hazardous occupations and processes.

CHILD PROTECTION:-
The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, is a comprehensive law that addresses the care and protection of children in conflict with the law and those in need of care and protection. It establishes a system for the rehabilitation of children in conflict with the law and focuses on their reform and reintegration.

CHILD MARRIGE:-
The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006, sets the legal age for marriage at 18 years for girls and 21 years for boys, aiming to prevent child marriages.

CHILD TRAFFICKING:-
India has various laws and mechanisms to combat child trafficking, including the Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, and the Protection of Children from Sexual Offenses (POCSO) Act, 2012, which addresses sexual exploitation and abuse of children.

NUTRITION AND HEALTH:-
The Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) program is one of the largest and most important initiatives for the welfare of children. It provides services like supplementary nutrition, immunization, and early childhood care to children under 6 years and pregnant and lactating mothers.

CHILD WELFARE ORGANIZATIONS:-
India has numerous governmental and non-governmental organizations working for the protection and welfare of children. The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) and State Commissions for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCRs) oversee the implementation of children's rights.

CHILD PARTICIPATION:-
Efforts have been made to promote children's participation in decisions affecting their lives. Child clubs and organizations have been formed to give children a platform to express their views.

INTERNATIONAL CONVENTION:-
India is a signatory to the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC), which sets out the rights and protections that should be afforded to children. India's policies and laws related to children's rights align with the principles of the CRC.
